1. Executive Summary

1.1 The Concept

Country Connection is a community-led, land-based wellness retreat for Aboriginal youth aged 13-18. The program combines a voluntary digital detox – a complete 5-day break from phones, social media, and screens – with immersive cultural learning led by Elders and Knowledge Keepers.

The core insight: You cannot take something away without putting something better in its place. We replace digital distraction with connection to Culture, Country, and Community.

1.2 The Problem Being Solved

Aboriginal youth spend an average of 6-9 hours per day on screens, leading to anxiety, depression, poor sleep, weakened cultural identity, and social isolation. Mainstream mental health services often fail to address these issues in culturally appropriate ways.

2. Vision & Mission

Vision: A future where Aboriginal youth carry the strength of their culture into a balanced digital world.

Mission: To empower Aboriginal youth by using cultural connection as the foundation for digital wellness, fostering resilience, identity, and community through land-based retreats led by Elders.

Core Values: Community-Led, Culture as Healing, Strength-Based, Trauma-Informed, Safe & Respectful.

3. Community Context & Need

Research indicates 86% of Indigenous youth use social media daily, 60% feel anxious when separated from their phone, and 45% are online past midnight. Local yarning circles confirm the need:

"I know I'm on my phone too much, but when I try to stop, I don't know what else to do." — Girl, 15
"My culture feels far away. Like something I'm supposed to care about but don't really know." — Boy, 16

4. Program Design & Philosophy

4.1 The Four Connections

  • Connect to Self: Journaling, meditation, letter to future self
  • Connect to Others: Traditional games, shared meals, teamwork
  • Connect to Culture: Ceremony, storytelling, craft-making
  • Connect to Country: Bush tucker walks, swimming, caring for Country

4.2 The Digital Detox Ceremony

Youth voluntarily place devices in a decorated box during an opening ceremony led by Elders – transforming surrender from a loss into a commitment.

6. Organizational Structure

Band Council (Oversight) → Elder's Council (Cultural Authority) → Retreat Director (Operations) → Cultural Mentors, Youth Workers, Coordinator, Cook, Mental Health (on-call).

Decision-making: Elders have full authority over cultural protocols. Retreat Director handles logistics and grants. Major changes require Band Council approval.

10. Funding Strategy & Grant Sources

Youth pay nothing. Funding mix: 50-70% government grants, 20-30% foundations, 10-20% corporate sponsorships, plus Band in-kind support.

Primary targets: Public Safety Canada (Crime Prevention Action Fund), Indigenous Services Canada (Mental Wellness), TELUS Friendly Future Foundation, MakeWay Foundation.

What we ask from Band Council: Permission for Elder's Council, help identifying location, letter of support – $0 from Band operating budget.

15. Staff Code of Conduct

  • Respect all cultural protocols as directed by Elders
  • Maintain confidentiality about youth disclosures
  • Model healthy digital behaviour (no phones during retreat)
  • Never be alone with a youth out of sight of others
  • Never use alcohol or drugs before or during retreat

16. Evaluation & Outcomes

Pre/Post Survey Questions: "I feel connected to my culture." "I feel stressed or worried most days." "I check my phone within 10 minutes of waking up."

Long-term tracking: 3-month follow-up calls, 6-month mailed surveys.
